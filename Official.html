Official

You found employ as a civil servant in the massive bureaucracy which runs the state. Most officials attain their rank by passing the imperial examinations, a grueling system of exams designed to test an applicant's knowledge of the classics and literary style. Others, however, come by their ranks through the nomination of a high ranking official. Whatever the case may be, you are a member of the gentry, well educated and intimately familiar with the mechanisms of power.

Skill Proficiencies: History, Investigation

Equipment: Robes indicating your rank, a record of your examination scores, common clothes, and 15 strings of silver coins.

Languages: One of your choice.

I became an official because:

d6  
1 I was groomed for this from a young age, studying the classics at the feet of private tutors.
2 I saw the corruption of the officials in my village and felt that I had a responsibility to do better.
3 It was the only real alternative to a life of backbreaking labor as a farmer.
4 I knew that securing a post in the bureaucracy would bring wealth and power to myself and those I loved.
5 In these chaotic times, everyone is responsible for upholding the law and brining stability to the land.
6 I studied the classics for pleasure, and took the examination only because it seemed convenient.

Certification

Most offices require that their bearers be certified by passing one of the six imperial examinations. Work with your DM to determine what certifications you possess, and what post they enabled you to hold in the bureaucracy. You can pick your certifications from the list below, or roll them randomly.

d6 Certification
1 Arithmetic
2 Calligraphy and Style
3 Classics and History
4 Law
5 Military Affairs
