Acolyte

You have spent your life in the service of a monastic order, following the Way of the Five-Fold Path. You act as an intermediary between the realm of the sacred and mundane, performing sacred rites and meditating on sutras. You are not necessarily a monk; not all monasteries instruct their acolytes in martial arts.

While serving their temple, acolytes typically take on vows of chastity, nonviolence, and poverty, and depend on the donations of local laypeople for survival. You should determine how strictly your character takes these prohibitions as an adventurer. While some acolytes are steadfast in their vows, many are willing to bend or reinterpret them when necessary, and some former acolytes renounce them entirely.

Skill Proficiencies: Insight, Religion

Equipment: A necklace of prayer beads, a book of sutras, 5 sticks of incense, monastic robes, a set of common clothes, and a beggar's bowl.

Languages: One of your choice.

I became an acolyte because:

d6  
1 I ran away from home at an early age and found refuge in a temple
2 My family gave me up to a temple, since they were unwilling or unable to care for me
3 I grew up in a household with strong religions convictions. Entering the order of the Five-Fold Path seemed natural.
4 The words of an itinerant monk struck a chord deep in my soul and moved me to devote myself to the Way.
5 I followed a childhood friend, a respected acquaintance, or someone I loved into religious service.
6 I was on the run from the law, and entered a monastic order in order to start a new life.
